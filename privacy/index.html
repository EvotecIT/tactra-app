Tactra Remote Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2, 2026

Tactra Remote is a local-first media remote for Home Assistant. It is designed to control your own media devices through your own Home Assistant instance and local network.

Data Tactra Handles

Tactra may store the following information on your devices:

  • Home Assistant server URL and instance name.
  • Home Assistant long-lived access token.
  • Selected media targets, favorite devices, activities, device preferences, control mappings, and app preferences.
  • Non-sensitive device snapshots used by widgets, App Intents, Shortcuts, Live Activities, and the Apple Watch companion.
  • Optional direct-provider endpoint information, such as local hostnames, IP addresses, ports, display names, and pairing status for supported local devices.

Sensitive Data

Home Assistant access tokens are stored locally in the Apple Keychain. Tactra does not intentionally sync Home Assistant access tokens through iCloud, widgets, App Intents, Watch snapshots, or app-group storage.

Local Network

Tactra can connect to Home Assistant and supported local media devices on your network. It may use local-network discovery protocols such as Bonjour/mDNS or SSDP/UPnP to find compatible devices. Local network data is used to show and control your own devices. Tactra does not send local network discovery results to a Tactra-operated cloud service.

Widgets, Shortcuts, and Apple Watch

Tactra uses an app group to share compact non-sensitive snapshots and preferences with widgets, App Intents, Shortcuts, Live Activities, and the Apple Watch companion. These snapshots may include device names, rooms, availability, playback state, volume state, source names, and activity names so Apple system surfaces can display useful controls.

iCloud Preference Sync

Tactra may offer manual iCloud key-value preference sync for non-sensitive settings, such as selected targets, favorites, activity preferences, device profiles, and direct endpoint metadata. Home Assistant access tokens are not included in this sync.

Data Collection by Tactra

Tactra does not require a Tactra account, does not operate an advertising network, does not sell your data, does not use your data for third-party tracking, and does not upload your Home Assistant token, device list, local network devices, or activity preferences to a Tactra-operated backend.

Apple Services

When you download, purchase, update, or test Tactra through Apple platforms, Apple may process purchase, download, crash, diagnostics, analytics, and TestFlight feedback information according to Apple's own policies and your Apple settings. If you enable iCloud preference sync, Apple provides the iCloud storage and transport for that Apple service.

Feedback and Support

If you contact support or send TestFlight feedback, the information you provide may include your email address, screenshots, crash logs, device model, operating system version, and details you choose to include. Support information is used to diagnose issues and improve Tactra.

Data Deletion

You can remove Tactra's local app data by deleting the app from your devices. You can revoke Home Assistant tokens in your Home Assistant profile. You can clear or replace saved Home Assistant instances and app preferences inside Tactra where supported.
